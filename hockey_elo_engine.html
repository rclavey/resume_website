How the Engine Works

- tuning trials

01

Build rosters

Parse every completed game into forward, defense, and goalie groups with time on ice and box-score production.

02

Update players

Share each team result across its lineup, then add centered individual impact from scoring, usage, plus-minus, and saves.

03

Rate teams

Combine roster means with tuned positional weights to produce the team Elo snapshot used before each game.

04

Add context

Calibrate game probabilities with home ice, rest, back-to-back status, and travel, preserving chronological order.

05

Simulate season

Replay the frozen schedule, seed each conference, and resolve best-of-seven rounds through the Stanley Cup.

Development process

How the Model Was Created

-
01

Historical game dataset

- completed NHL games were processed in chronological order. Each row includes lineups, position groups, time on ice, scoring, plus-minus, shots faced, and saves.

02

Time-aware evaluation

Earlier seasons train the ratings, - form walk-forward validation folds, and - remains the final holdout. Future games never influence earlier ratings.

03

Bayesian parameter search

- seeded Optuna TPE trials search positional weights, team and individual K factors, performance weights, scales, and season regression.

04

Stable production selection

The objective minimizes mean validation log loss plus a - stability penalty. The best - candidates are then compared on holdout generalization.

Rating construction

How Player and Team Scores Are Built

One chronological update per game
1

Initialize player ratings

Every previously unseen player begins at 1000 Elo. At a new season, existing ratings regress toward the current player mean.

2

Build the pregame team score

Average the current Elo of dressed forwards, defensemen, and the starting goalie, then combine those groups using the tuned weights shown below.

Team Elo = - F + - D + - G
3

Convert the gap to an expectation

The two team ratings enter the standard 400-point Elo curve to estimate each team's pregame expected result.

E_home = 1 / (1 + 10 ^ ((R_away - R_home) / 400))
4

Apply the shared team result

Players receive a position-specific team update from the difference between the actual result and the expected result.

Team component = K_position x (actual - expected)
5

Add individual game impact

Within each position group, performance is centered around teammates, bounded with tanh, and added to the shared result so individual credit cannot dominate the outcome.

6

Carry ratings forward

The updated player ratings become the input to the next chronological game. Team ratings are rebuilt from the players dressing for that game rather than stored as permanent club ratings.
