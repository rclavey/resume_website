How the Model Works

Chronological player-weighted Elo

01

Game rosters

Each historical game supplies the participating players and their seconds played.

02

Team strength

Pregame team Elo is the mean rating of the players who appeared for that team.

03

Player updates

The team result creates an Elo change that is distributed by each player's share of minutes.

04

Tournament forecast

Frozen roster ratings drive neutral-court game probabilities and complete bracket simulations.

Team Elo

Active roster average

R_team = mean(R_player)

A team inherits the average strength of the players who actually appeared, so roster composition changes the pregame rating.

Win probability

Standard Elo expectation

P(A) = 1 / (1 + 10^((R_B - R_A) / 400))

Every bracket game is treated as neutral court and converted from the frozen team-rating difference.

Team update

Result surprise

ΔR_team = K × (result - expected)

Unexpected wins create larger positive changes; expected outcomes create smaller changes.

Player update

Minutes-weighted allocation

ΔR_player = ΔR_team × roster_size × minutes_share

Players with more time on court receive more of the team-level Elo movement.

Important Assumptions

Frozen ratings

Future tournament games do not update Elo inside the projected bracket or Monte Carlo runs.

Neutral court

No home-court adjustment is included in tournament matchup probabilities.

Roster continuity

The latest pre-tournament roster snapshot stands in for the available tournament lineup.

Independent simulation draws

Each unresolved game is sampled from its Elo probability; injuries and matchup-specific effects are outside the model.
